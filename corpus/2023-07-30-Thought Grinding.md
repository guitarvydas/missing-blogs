# 2023-07-30-Thought GrindingWhen we want to make an elevator pitch, we take a thought and grind it and buff it until it becomes more concise and meaningful using as few words as possible.

This is often done in audio recording of music. The recording engineer selects a region of music for a "punch-in". The performer performs the same part over and over again.  Every performance snippet is recorded. The engineer, the producer, and, the performer choose the best take and discard the rest.

In editing text, we want to do something similar. We want to write our thoughts, come as they may. Then we want to refinez the thoughts by editing. We want to edit the thought over and over again until it has a clear and concise phrasing.  Until it becomes as good as an elevator pitch.

AI speech-to-text tools can help with this process. We can record our thoughts without editing and have AI convert the text, and, display the text back to us. We can read the captured text aloud, adding new bits and small edits as we go. We end up with a file containing several takes of the same thought. Then, using a standard text editor, we can select the best version and discard the rest.

Currently, I'm experimenting with using the Drafts app on my iPhone to capture and punch-in my thoughts. Then, I use Obsidian to do the final edit.
