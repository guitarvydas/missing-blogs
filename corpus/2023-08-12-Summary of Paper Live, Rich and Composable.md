# Summary

The document discusses qualities that are important for programming beyond static text. It identifies three main qualities - liveness, richness, and composability. Liveness refers to providing live feedback about a program's dynamic behavior as it is edited. Richness allows programming through domain-specific representations and interactions rather than just text. Composability is the ability to combine smaller programmed artifacts into larger ones.   
  
Traditional programming relies on static text and lacks liveness and richness. Many efforts have aimed to add these qualities, but often at the cost of composability. The document argues that combining all three qualities - liveness, richness, and composability - can better capture the full potential of interactive computation without losing what makes programming expressive.   
  
It examines examples that have liveness without richness, and richness without liveness. Systems that combine liveness and richness often do so without composability, limiting their usefulness. Programming by demonstration systems exemplify live and rich tools.  
  
The document discusses challenges in achieving composability when combining liveness and richness. Approaches like manual integration and pre-made bundles fail to provide open-ended composability. Recent systems like mage and livelits address this by extending environments like Jupyter notebooks and Hazel with live and rich tooling, achieving all three qualities.  
  
This allows domain-specific interactive programming tools to be integrated into general-purpose environments with full liveness. The document argues this can unlock new value for live and rich tools and programming research by enabling composition. It lays out a vision for further exploring the design space at the intersection of liveness, richness, and composability.
