# 2023-05-16-Essential Complexity is DefeatismSaying that a problem is *essentially complex* is a defeatist attitude.

The job of Academics is 
1. to spend time gaining an understanding about something, then, 
2. to explain that understanding to others in a simple way.  

Many Academics succeed at (1) but then ignore (2) and write the understanding out in a notation called Mathematics.  Mathematics notation does not appear simple to most people, and, this, therefore, constitutes failure of the academic.  Doing (1), but not doing (2), is only half the job.  Doing only half the job is failure.

Another way of thinking about this is to note that Mathematics notation has a sweet spot.  Some things are naturally easy to understand when written out in Mathematical notation.  Other explanations miss the sweet spot of Mathematics Notation and cause State Explosion, also known as "bloatware" in software.  

An example of State Explosion is the use of Mathematical Notation (called Functional Programming in software) to explain multitasking.  Multitasking is a simple concept - it can be understood - but, multitasking appears to be complex when explained using *only* Mathematical Notation.  This insistence on explaining multitasking using only a *single* notation leads to epicycles and bloatware.

What is the sweet spot of Mathematics?  It behooves us to remember what the *simplifying assumption* of Mathematical notation is: ignore the dimension of *time*.  The conclusion is obvious: one shouldn't use Mathematical Notation to describe anything that involves the dimension of *time*, e.g. something like seqencing and multitasking.  

Yes, it is *possible* to explain sequencing and multitasking in Mathematical Notation, but, that is just a stupid pet trick[^1] that appeals to cultists and notation worshippers.

[1] David Letterman, "Late Night with David Letterman".

The suggestion is: change notations, use multiple notations.  Fervor in trying to generalize everything and to create a unified notation leads to State Explosion and epicycles and bloatware.

Put yet another way - as offensive as possible - might be to say that Functional Programming is a *fad*.
