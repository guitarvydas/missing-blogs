# 2023-07-21-UNDO Aint Good EnoughThinking out loud:

- I wish I had a tool that tracked all of my development moves and reminded me (tomorrow, next week?) of what I was doing.

- Git doesn’t do this, you have to break Flow to remember to ask git to save intermediate work.

- Git is built for Waterfall development - you are encouraged to know ahead of time what your plan of development is, e.g. bug fix, feature improvement

- but, for an iterative development cycle, you go back and forth and wipe out work as you learn, the result is a bunch of branches, not one linear branch for development of a feature

- UNDO is too linear for this kind of approach, too.  You can remember what 1 UNDO step undoes, but what if you want to UNDO 10 steps or to a few days ago?  You CAN do this with UNDO, but, you have to be careful and remember where you were in the past.

- Emacs’ UNDO is especially bad, it requires 2 keypresses for each UNDO, if you make a mistake, it begins to UNDO your UNDOs and becomes very confusing.
