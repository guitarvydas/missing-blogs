# 2023-07-11-First New UI Paradigm In 60 Years?... Reading and thinking about: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ai-paradigm/

The above article claims that AI is the first New GUI paradigm in 60 years.

I think that the phrase "..tell the computer what they want, not how to do it..." does not capture the difference between software-GUIs and AI-based interaction.  

This phrasing simply means "Declarative programming".

Declarative programming has been around for decades. We've seen declarative programming in many forms: 
- HTML
- PROLOG and other Relational programming languages
- Spreadsheets
- Barliman[^Barliman]
- etc.  
- (but not HTML+JS).  

Declarative programming languages allow the language *engine* to figure out how to produce a result.

IMO, the real difference between declarative programming and AI-based interaction is the degree of precision needed in the requirements.  

Most programming languages, including Relational languages, require niggly amounts of detail in the specification.  GPT produces results without needing so much niggly detail.  In fact, the input specification can be ambiguous, culminating in "natural language".  

The difference is observed when the user *doesn't* provide enough detail.  In previous approaches, the programmers see error messages, or worse, a confusing lack of results or deep looping.  In GPT, the engine infers what is specified and generates *something,* even if it's wrong or even if it's BS. 

Engineers and Academics think about precise details. Other kinds of humans skip over details and give specifications that are loosey-goosey.

## Numbers - Simple Example

Here is a simple example of two ways to think about problems...

Non-programmers like the concept of *numbers*.  That's what spreadsheets give them.

Rigorists want to specify more detail, e.g. a number is specified as:
- an integer
- a float
- a double
- an int8, int16, int32, ...
- unsigned vs. signed
- bignum vs. limited size
- decimal or binary
- etc.
