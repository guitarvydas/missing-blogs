# 2023-05-28-Software Engineering vs GPT vs SCNsYes, ChatGPT can *implement* software.

ChatGPT can write *code*.  Being trained only in one pattern, though, I find it hard to believe that ChatGPT will write anything but *synchronous* code.

I believe that the synchronous-only pattern limits our imagination of what "computers" (electronic machines) can be used for.

Engineering is about *thinking about* and *designing* code, not writing it.  Engineering is about *control* and *predictability* of outcomes.

In essence, Software Architecture is like requirements gathering and fitting a solution to a specific need.  Engineering is the process of making all of the trade-offs necessary to make the required design work.  Production Engineering - a subset of Engineering - is the process of making more trade-offs to make an already-working design "better" in some way - cheaper, faster, smaller footprint, etc.

ChatGPT is a robot.  It can only do what it has been told to do. CharGPT looks magical at this moment. At one time, QWERTY keyboards seemed magical, too.  

Yet, QWERTY keyboards don't necessarily make a design correct nor make it better.  If the requirements are buggy, the resulting code will be buggy, but, nonetheless, the resulting code will strictly adhere to the (buggy) requirements.

SCNs (Solution-Centric Notations) are not only about writing code.  SCNs are for ratcheting up thought.  If a problem is deemed to be solved, a new SCN skin can be developed to solve an even greater problem.

Better input devices - keyboards, WX7 wind controllers, ChatGPT, etc. - can help humans solve certain problems and make those problems seem mundane, i.e. "low level".  As soon as a problem seems mundane, human creativity can be ratched upwards to solve even greater problems using newly-low-level-ized concepts.

Humanity has undergone at least several sea-changes in thought, for example
- perspective - once the notion of *perspectve* was introduced, Art took on a different flavour.  Flat Art suddenly became non-flat.  In essence, humans began to routinely observe effects of a 3rd dimension.
- Earth goes around the Sun - for several thousands of years, humanity believed that the Universe revolved around the Earth, and futzed observations of Reality to fit this belief.  The futzes were called "epicycles".  Then "suddenly" humanity shifted from Ptolemaic Cosmology to Copernican Cosmology and shed the cosmological epicycles that had been created.  Once freed from having to spend time and effort to futz observations, humanity began "seeing" new opportunities for research and understanding.  Travel to other planets became thinkable.  Prior to this shift in thinking, it was fundamentally impossible to imagine travel to other planets.  The idea of other planets simply could not be conceived.

ChatGPT is a tool, like a hammer.  It can augment human ingenuity.  The printing press was a tool for humans.  The use of pointy sticks and clay tablets were tools for humans.  The use of graphite-tipped sticks and rubber and paper were tools for humans.  Now we have electronic machines (aka "computers") and we are allowed to think in new ways.

We can lay down and let the tools command our actions, or, we can use the tools like tools and ratchet up our abilities by solving new problems.

SCNs are not a technology, they are a way of life.

## Caveats
### What If I'm Wrong About The Capabilities of ChatGPT?
My favourite songwriting professor (Pat Pattison) said "the more you know, the more creative you can be".

What if I had *perfect* memory and *perfect* random access to *all* knowledge?  Would I be more creative?  Would I still be able to create new knowledge?

Does *perfect* random access to *all* knowledge become a hindrance at some point?  Does that knowledge need to be hierachicalized / structured in some manner?

### Are We At The Cusp of a New Way of Seeing Reality?
We, currently, see Reality in 4D - X, Y, Z, t.  What if there are other dimensions that we don't - yet - perceive?

There are signs that humanity is "ignoring" certain observations about Reality.  So-called scientists are simply ignoring certain facts.  

For example, the book "Babylon's Banksters - The Alchemy of Deep Physics, High Finance and Ancient Religion" quotes a Wall-Street (I didn't take notes, so I am unsure if this is exact) study that showed a strong correlation between planetary positions and the markets.  Scientists don't wish to explain this correlation and simply ignore it (at least within the domain of public knowledge).

The relatively new movement called "Flat Earthers" seems to present good explanations for many phenomena that we feel we understand through science.  Flat Earthers do not argue that the Earth is flat, only that it is not a round ball.  

I wonder what perspective-ers sounded like to flat-ers in days of yore?

The shift from Ptolemaic Cosmology to Copernican Cosmology (actually, Gallilean, I think) is documented in the book "Sleepwalkers" by Arthur Koestler.  That shift overturned 1,000's of years of belief structure in only a few generations.

Would we even know / detect that we are making a shift in understanding?

If we *are* making a shift, what is GPT?  Does GPT represent the shift, or, is GPT just a repository for the "old way" of thinking in 4D?
