# GOTO

GOTO v1 - assembler GOTO
GOTO v2 - message passing
GOTO v3 - CPS - Continuation Passing Style
GOTO v4 - URL

All versions of GOTO suffer from the problem of so-called "structured"-ness.  I am searching for more descriptive words.  I'm considering words like "nesting" (thinking of Russian Dolls) and "isolation" (more-than "encapsulation" ; "isolation" includes control-flow and not just data).

GOTO v1 and v3 are concerned with control flow within a single machine.  AKA synchronous.

GOTO v2 and v4 are concerned with control flow across a network of distributed machines.  AKA asynchronous.

## Random Notes

- Kinopio is GOTO v4
	- I love Kinopio
	- I used to love Scapple, but, I love Kinopio even more
- Message Passing, using "isolation", using something like Structured Message Passing (I'm thinking of Org Charts) is my current sweet spot.
- most current *programming languages* are based on the Synchronous Pattern, i.e. they can handle GOTO v1 and GOTO v3 well, but don't handle GOTO v2 and GOTO v4 very well (they *can* handle these concepts, but not well)
- "come from" is just GOTO in reverse, i.e. *backlinks*
 
### Lessons From Org Charts
- send *commands* (requests) downwards
- expect *summaries* to be returned upwards (filtered info)
- multiple layers of *middle management*
- no "going over the boss's head"
- no "micromanagement"
- scalability
	- successful business can scale
	- unsuccessful businesses - cottage industry businesses - do not scale
